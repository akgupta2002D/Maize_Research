Finding Localizing Amino Acid Sequences in Proteins

Below are the resources and tools used for identifying localizing amino acid sequences in proteins.

Protein Localization Tools

  • MaizeGDB - The Maize Genetics and Genomics Database. Use this to search for genes and find protein sequences.
    https://www.maizegdb.org/
  • NLS Mapper - Predicts nuclear localization signals in proteins.
    https://nls-mapper.iab.keio.ac.jp/cgi-bin/NLS_Mapper_form.cgi
  • NoLS (Dundee) - Identifies nucleolar localization signals.
    https://www.compbio.dundee.ac.uk/www-nod/
  • TargetP-2.0 - Predicts subcellular localization of proteins, specifically for plants.
    https://services.healthtech.dtu.dk/service.php?TargetP-2.0
  • DeepMito - Predicts mitochondrial localization signals in proteins.
    https://busca.biocomp.unibo.it/deepmito/

Instructions

  1. Use the gene ID to search for the gene on MaizeGDB.
    • Note: Version 4 IDs start with 'Zm' and have a 'd' in the middle. Prefer using V4 IDs as they are more up-to-date.
  2. Find the protein sequence of the canonical transcript.
    • Select the protein made by the canonical transcript (not always T001).
  3. Use the protein sequence in the following prediction software:
    • NLS Mapper - For nuclear localization signals.
    • NoLS Dundee - For nucleolar localization signals.
    • TargetP-2.0 - For general localization (select 'plants').
    • DeepMito - For mitochondrial localization signals.
  4. Save the results and include them in your report.
    • You can create a table summarizing the findings and include screenshots or images of the results.
